History

I was born in Dublin, California in 1988. I received my high school diploma in 2006 from Portola High School in eastern California. Four years later, I completed my Bachelor of Science degree in physics with Highest Honors at the University of California, Davis. I joined the Physics Department at the University of Washington in 2012; for more information on my experience and qualifications, visit the experience page.

The Hilltop neighborhood of Tacoma is where I call home, along with my brother, William.

Electronic Entertainment

When the opportunity arises, I delight in playing video games, especially with friends. One of my top favorites is Kerbal Space Program. It's unique among video games: An earth-and-space simulator, accessible for those of all backgrounds. You build and fly your own planes and rockets, put together like LEGOs--one piece at a time. From landing modules to space stations, it's a great way to indulge your creative side.

Sending little green people on missions to another planet, even in a virtual universe, is a very rewarding experience. Shown below are my Kerbals commemorating a successful landing on Tylo, a moon of the gas giant Jool.

Outdoors

I find hiking, climbing, and kayaking to be fantastic ways to unwind and de-stress. Few surroundings instill a feeling of peace as does the calm of a lush, green forest, or a still lake echoing with birdsong. I make frequent trips to enjoy the nearby Cascade mountains and Olympic mountains.

Living near connected waterways in Seattle affords plenty of opportunity to explore the city from the water. Graduate studies are very busy work, but when my schedule permits it, I like to paddle around.
